Pizza

Of Neapolitan pizza there is only one with immediately recognizable characteristics, to the eye and to the palate:

  • the high, crisp edge, just like bread
  • the blood red tomato
  • evo oil in a perfect emulsion
  • fresh basil
  • the melted mozzarella

These are the "few and simple" ingredients that make up the recipe for true Neapolitan pizza, not one more, not one less.

It all sounds very easy, doesn't it? Yet it isn't!

Because to make a Neapolitan pizza as tradition dictates, you need top-quality ingredients, certainly, but you also need skilled hands, experience, professionalism. These are all things that are acquired over the years thanks to a wealth of work experience that adds up to a base of high-level professional training.

Ingredients List

  • brewer's yeast
  • Wheat flour 0
  • Whole-wheat flours from different sources (depending on taste)
  • pulp of peeled tomatoes (freshly prepared)
  • fior di latte or buffalo mozzarella cheese
  • fresh basil
  • evo oil

Steps

  1. Mix the brewer's yeast into the water, which must be at room temperature; then, that mixture of water and yeast should be added flush to the flour mix, trying to incorporate it gently to avoid lumps.
  2. The dough should then be put to rise inside a large bowl covered with a cloth for as long as necessary (at least 10 hours); after that, the dough should be broken into buns, rolled out into a regular ball and left to rest for another 8 hours to conclude the pizza rising process.
  3. At this point, the Neapolitan pizza dough loaf is ready: it should be rolled out on a floured surface in the classic motion starting from the center and going outward.
  4. Add the topping of your choise
  5. Bake the pizza in a wood-fired oven at a temperature of 500 degrees
